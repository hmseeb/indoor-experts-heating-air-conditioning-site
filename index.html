FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about HVAC services in Atlanta.

How much does it cost to replace an AC unit in Atlanta?
+
The cost to replace an AC unit in Atlanta varies based on the unit's size, brand, and installation specifics. On average, homeowners can expect to pay between $3,000 to $7,000 for a complete replacement. Contact us for a free in-home estimate.
When should I replace my AC unit?
+
The best time to replace your AC unit is when it is over 10-15 years old, frequently requires repairs, or fails to adequately cool your home, indicating inefficiency or potential failure. Indoor Experts offers expert assessments to help you make the right decision.
How long does HVAC installation take in Atlanta?
+
The HVAC installation process in Atlanta typically takes between 6-8 hours for most homes, depending on the system and complexity. We complete each job with attention to detail, leaving your home clean and your system operating smoothly.
Can I finance my HVAC system installation?
+
Yes! Indoor Experts offers flexible financing options for HVAC system installation in Atlanta. We work with Wisetack, Synchrony, and Okinus to provide affordable monthly payment plans, including 0% interest for up to 24 months.
How often should I service my air conditioner?
+
It's recommended to service your air conditioner at least once a year, preferably before the cooling season, to ensure it runs efficiently and reliably. Our 5-Star Maintenance Club handles this automatically for you.
What are the benefits of regular HVAC maintenance?
+
Regular HVAC maintenance includes improved energy efficiency, extended system lifespan, reduced repair costs, and enhanced indoor air quality. Regular servicing helps identify potential issues early and ensures optimal performance.
Are there hidden fees for your HVAC services?
+
No. At Indoor Experts, we prioritize transparency and ensure that all costs are clearly communicated upfront. You know exactly what to expect before we start any work.
What energy efficiency ratings should I look for?
+
Look for the Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio (SEER) for air conditioners and the Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency (AFUE) for furnaces. Higher ratings indicate better efficiency and lower energy costs. Our team will guide you to the best option.
